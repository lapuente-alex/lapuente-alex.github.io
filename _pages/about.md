Hello! I'm a second year PhD student at Boston University interested in experimental observational cosmology. I currenlty work on millimeter-wave Line Intensity Mapping (LIM) in the SuperSpec and SPT-SLIM collaborations. This research program uses Kinetic Inductance Detectors (KIDs) to observe atomic and molecular emission spectra from galaxies at high redshift (z ∼ 0.5–5), surveying the universe’s large-scale structure. This technique is uniquely poised to complement existing cosmological surveys and produce precise constraints on cosmological parameters.

Within my collaboration groups, I'm currently most focused on analysis from our first deployments (flux calibration, noise characterization, data reduction, principal component analysis, etc.). In the future, I hope to use our forthcoming deployments' datasets to better understand how matter behaves on very large scales (and thus, indirectly, on very small scales).

On this site, you can currently find links to my CV and other pages. At [this link](https://physics.bu.edu/~lapuente/), you can find some more technical summaries for my collaborations.
